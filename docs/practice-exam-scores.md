# Practice Exam Scores — "Am I Ready?"

## How should I interpret my practice exam scores?

> **Understanding your score matters less than understanding *why* you got questions wrong.** Practice exams exist to find gaps in your knowledge — that's the entire point. A low score on your first attempt is normal and expected.

## What do the different score ranges mean?

| Score | What it likely means |
|-------|----------------------|
| **60%+** | OK, but you may be rushing. Review domain-level gaps carefully. |
| **70%+** | Getting there. Dig into the 30% you got wrong. |
| **80%+** | You may know the subject well — or you may be memorizing answers. Could be ready. |

No magic number guarantees a pass. Some people pass with 70%s; others fail with 85%s.

!!! tip "Review everything"
    After each exam, review **every flagged and incorrect question**. Understand *why* the right answer is right and *why* your choice was wrong. Also review questions you guessed correctly — a lucky guess is still a gap.

## Which practice exam provider is most realistic?

It varies. The exam question bank is massive, so one person might find Tutorials Dojo closer to the real thing while another swears by Udemy. Use multiple sources, and don't read too much into which one "feels" closest.

## Is it bad to fail my first few practice exams?

No. Failing early practice exams is common — you're learning the exam technique as much as the content. What matters is that your scores trend **upward over time**.

!!! warning "Don't just memorize answers"
    If your scores improve because you're remembering answers from previous attempts, the improvement is fake. The first pass through a question bank is the most valuable one. Subsequent passes risk memorization, not learning.

## What's more important than the score itself?

**The learning from reviewing your mistakes.** Each incorrect or flagged question is a signal. Trace it back to the underlying domain or service, study that topic, and move on. This process, repeated across many practice exams, is what prepares you for the real thing.

## The bottom line

- Practice exams are a **diagnostic tool**, not a pass/fail prediction.
- Analyze your *trend* across exams, not any single score.
- Review wrong answers **and** lucky guesses.
- If you're consistently scoring 75–80%+ across multiple providers and you understand *why* every answer is right, you're likely ready.
